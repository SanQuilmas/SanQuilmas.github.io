Hobbys

Reading

I read a lot of serialized fiction on Royal Road. Here are some of my top recommendations:
  • Systema Delenda Est
  • Mother of Learning
  • Rock Falls, Everyone Dies
  • Dungeon of Knowledge
  • 1% Lifesteal
  • His Soul is Marching On
  • The Stubborn Skill Grinder in a Time Loop
  • The Stars Have Eyes

I also recommend anything written by these authors:

  • InadvisablyCompelled
  • R.C. Joshua

Outside of web fiction, I also enjoy reading traditional novels. Some of my favorites include:

  • Anything by Greg Egan — brilliant, mind-expanding hard sci-fi that's deeply influenced how I think about science and storytelling.
  • The Harry Dresden series by Jim Butcher — modern fantasy fun.
  • The Name of the Wind by Patrick Rothfuss — still waiting on that final book...
  • The Remembrance of Earth's Past series by Liu Cixin — mind-blowing sci-fi.
  • The Belgariad by David Eddings — a fun, nostalgic ride. Classic fantasy feel
  • Yes, I still haven't read The Lord of the Rings — it's on the list!
  • Adrian Tchaikovsky — consistently delivers excellent speculative fiction.
  • James Patterson's romance novels — a guilty pleasure of mine.
  • Donde los Árboles Cantan by Laura Gallego García — a beautifully written Spanish-language fantasy with rich worldbuilding and emotional depth.
  • The Magic 2.0 series by Scott Meyer — time-traveling wizard programmers. It's as fun and nerdy as it sounds.
  • The Frankenstein series by Dean Koontz — surprisingly engaging despite my usual dislike for Koontz. I really enjoyed the first books, though the last half of the final one lost me. Not a strong recommendation, but definitely entertaining.
  • Please Don't Tell My Parents I'm a Supervillain by Richard Roberts — an absolute blast. Superpowered teens, moral ambiguity, and tons of fun.
  • Ciaphas Cain series — top-tier Warhammer fiction (runner-up: the Mechanicum series).
